Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the sun.

Mars is the seventh planet in size.

Named after Mars, the ancient Roman god of war.

Mars is also called the "red planet" because of the reddish hue of the surface.

Mars is a terrestrial planet with a rarefied atmosphere (the pressure on the surface is 160 times less than that of the earth).

Mars has two natural satellites - Phobos and Deimos, translated from ancient Greek - "fear" and "horror".

This is a photo of Mars.

The relief of Mars has many unique features. The Martian extinct volcano Mount Olympus is the highest known mountain on the planets of the solar system.

The northern hemispheres of the planet are radically different in relief; there is a hypothesis that the Great Northern Plain, which occupies 40% of the planet's surface, is an impact crater; in this case, it turns out to be the largest known impact crater in the solar system.

In July 2021, The Hope probe, sent by the United Arab Emirates to study the Martian atmosphere, released images of the planet's aurora. This discovery was unexpected and exciting, but at the same time the question arose: how is the formation of auroras possible on a planet where there is no global magnetic field? Now physicists have solved this mystery.

As you know, auroras arise due to the solar wind - a stream of ionized particles that come from the Sun at a speed of 300-1200 km / s. When they reach the Earth's magnetic field, they merge with the atmosphere, as a result of which we see a bright colorful phenomenon.

The aurora borealis on Mars is a celestial light that appears mainly in the southern hemisphere of the planet.

The new results complement a study published last year and show that a proton aurora is emerging on Mars.
