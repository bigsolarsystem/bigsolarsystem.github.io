Mercury

Mercury is the first planet from the sun and the smallest planet in the solar system.

Named after the ancient Roman god of trade.

Its period of revolution around the Sun is only 87.97 Earth days - the shortest among all the planets in the solar system.

In a telescope, Mercury can see phases that vary from a thin crescent to an almost full disk, like Venus and the Moon, and sometimes it passes over the disk of the Sun.

Mercury's axis has the smallest tilt of all the planets in the solar system (about 1/30 of a degree).

However, its orbital eccentricity is maximum.

Orbital eccentricity is a numerical characteristic of the orbit of a celestial body (or spacecraft), which characterizes the "compression" of the orbit. In general, the orbit of a celestial body is a conic section (that is, an ellipse, parabola, hyperbola, or straight line), and the eccentricity of the orbit is the eccentricity of the corresponding curve. The orbits of many bodies in the solar system are ellipses.

The temperature of its surface varies more than on any other planet in the solar system: from 100 K (−173 ° C) at night to 700 K (+427 ° C) during the day in the equatorial regions.

Mercury has no satellites.

Mercury was visited by two spacecraft: in 1974 and 1975, Mariner 10 flew near it, and from 2008 to 2015 it was explored by MESSENGER. The latter in 2011 went into orbit around the planet and, having made more than 4,000 orbits around it in four years, on April 30, 2015, it used up fuel and crashed into the surface.

The BepiColombo spacecraft is scheduled to arrive at Mercury in 2025.
