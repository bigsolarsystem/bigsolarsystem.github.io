Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet in terms of distance from the Sun and the second largest planet in the solar system after Jupiter. Saturn is classified as a gas giant planet. Saturn is named after the Roman god of agriculture.

Saturn is mostly made up of hydrogen, with some helium and traces of water, methane, ammonia, and heavy elements. The inner region is a relatively small core of iron, nickel and ice, covered with a thin layer of metallic hydrogen and a gaseous outer layer.

Saturn has a planetary magnetic field, which occupies an intermediate position in strength between the Earth's magnetic field and the powerful field of Jupiter.

Saturn has a prominent ring system, consisting mainly of ice particles, a smaller amount of heavy elements and dust.
