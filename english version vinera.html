Venus

Venus is the second planet from the sun.

Venus is the sixth largest planet in the solar system, along with Mercury, Earth and Mars, belonging to the family of terrestrial planets.

Named after the ancient Roman goddess of love, Venus.

According to a number of characteristics - for example, in mass and size - Venus is considered the "sister" of the Earth.

Venus has no natural satellites. It is the third brightest object in the Earth's sky, after the Sun and Moon. The planet reaches an apparent magnitude of −4.6m, so it is bright enough to cast shadows at night. Occasionally, Venus is visible to the naked eye and during daylight hours.

Venus has a dense atmosphere composed of more than 96% carbon dioxide. Atmospheric pressure on the surface of the planet is 92 times greater than on the surface of the Earth, and is approximately equal to the pressure of water at a depth of 900 meters. Due to the high pressure, carbon dioxide in the near-surface part of the atmosphere is no longer a gas, but a supercritical fluid, so this part of the atmosphere is a “semi-liquid-semi-gaseous” ocean of supercritical carbon dioxide.

Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system, with an average surface temperature of 735 K (462°C), even though Mercury is closer to the Sun.
